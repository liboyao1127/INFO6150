Understanding Glaucoma

What is Glaucoma?

Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, crucial for good vision. This damage is often caused by abnormally high pressure in your eye.

Recognizing Glaucoma

  • Severe eye pain
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Sudden onset of visual disturbance
  • Blurred vision
  • Halo around lights
  • Redness in the eye

Caring for Yourself with Glaucoma

Regular eye check-ups, adhering to prescribed treatment, and monitoring eye health can help manage symptoms and slow progression.

For more information, visit your local eye care professional.
